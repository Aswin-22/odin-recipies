Onigiri Is A Common Japanese Food

Onigiri are Japanese rice balls, which appear in a couple of Naruto's scenes. One such moment takes place when Sakura and Sasuke are kids. Sakura stares at Sasuke from a far, watching him eat his Onigiri.


About Onigiri

Onigiri, also known as omusubi, nigirimeshi, or rice ball, is a Japanese food made from white rice formed into triangular or cylindrical shapes and often wrapped in nori.

Onigiri Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Furikake
  • Nori

Steps to make Onigiri

  1. Pop your cooked rice into a nice mixing bowl. Add the furikake or rice flavouring and mix through evenly. Separate
    the rice into equal portions, big enough to be a large handful each.
  2. Wet your hands with water and rub together with a pinch or two of salt. This stops the rice sticking to your
    hands and helps keep it fresher for longer.
  3. Take up one portion of rice in your hands.
  4. If you are hiding some fillings inside, here is where you make an indent, place the ingredients inside and fold
    the rice over, then lightly press into a ball.
  5. Using mainly your fingertips while resting the rice on your palm, start to press and squeeze the rice into a triangular shape,
